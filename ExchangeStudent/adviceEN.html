Advice from experience:


So, I would like to share with you some advice in order for you to complete this challenge with the biggest amount of return...


Number one


Have present in your mind that when you tell your goal to someone else, your brain get's the exact same feeling it would get as if you already completed the challenge. That's why we read the goal statement every morning and night. Check out the TED talk over here!


Number two


Studying while doing this challenge is completely optional. To the date I've never studied my notebook and the most useful words and sentences stayed with me through the process of repetition. That means the more you need that word the more times you'll find yourself asking again "How do you say (word) in (language you want to learn)" and through that process believe me, they will stay. Because who likes looking like a fool?


Things not to do


  • Asking for someone to translate without you being next to them processing the information they're sharing with you. (Believe me, those sentences will not stay)
  • Mistaking temporal failure with total failure (Keep going!)
  • Don’t say a sentence in (Language you want to stop speaking) if you already have it in your notebook. If you want to say it look for it and read it.

Last but not least


Never, never give up.

Think about it, what's the worst thing that could happen? You may even find out what you're capable of.

Just trust the process, stick to the plan and DON'T STOP.
